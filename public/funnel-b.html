Face procedures
Which procedure are you considering?
Not sure of the exact name? The descriptions below will help. Many people come to us unsure — that's exactly what we're here for.
Rhinoplasty
Also known as: nose job, nose reshaping
Reshaping the nose for proportion, symmetry or to improve breathing. One of the most nuanced procedures — surgeon selection is critical.
›
Blepharoplasty
Also known as: eyelid surgery, eye lift, upper or lower lid surgery
Removes excess skin or fat from the upper or lower eyelids to create a more rested, open appearance.
›
Brow Lift / Forehead Lift
Also known as: browplasty, temporal lift
Raises the brows and smooths forehead lines to reduce a tired or heavy appearance. Often combined with eyelid surgery.
›
Facelift / Neck Lift
Also known as: rhytidectomy, lower face rejuvenation
Tightens and lifts sagging skin on the face, jowls and neck. Results can be dramatic or subtle depending on technique and goals.
›
Fat Transfer to Face
Also known as: fat grafting, facial volumisation, surgical filler
Uses your own fat to restore volume to hollowed cheeks, temples or under-eyes. Natural, long-lasting results.
›
Ear Reshaping
Also known as: otoplasty, ear pinning, ear correction
Reshapes or repositions prominent or asymmetric ears. Common in both adults and children, with a high satisfaction rate.
›
Chin / Jaw Augmentation
Also known as: chin implant, genioplasty, jaw reshaping
Enhances chin projection or jaw definition for improved facial balance and proportion.
›

Body procedures
Which procedure are you considering?
Not sure of the exact name? Read the descriptions — many of these overlap and your surgeon will help confirm the right approach.
Tummy Tuck
Also known as: abdominoplasty, flat tummy surgery
Removes excess skin and tightens abdominal muscles. Common after pregnancy, significant weight loss, or where diet and exercise haven't achieved the result.
›
Liposuction
Also known as: lipo, fat removal, body sculpting
Removes stubborn fat deposits from targeted areas. Works best on localised fat rather than overall weight loss — skin elasticity is an important factor.
›
Body Contouring / Sculpting
Also known as: body sculpting, hi-def lipo, 360 lipo
A more comprehensive approach to reshaping the body — often combining lipo with skin tightening or muscle definition techniques.
›
Arm Lift
Also known as: brachioplasty, bat wing correction
Removes excess skin and fat from the upper arms. Often sought after significant weight loss when skin has lost its elasticity.
›
Thigh Lift
Also known as: thighplasty, inner thigh lift
Tightens and reshapes the inner or outer thighs by removing excess skin. Often combined with liposuction for best results.
›
Brazilian Butt Lift (BBL)
Also known as: fat transfer to buttocks, bum augmentation
Uses your own fat (harvested via liposuction) to enhance the shape and projection of the buttocks. Safety and surgeon experience are paramount here.
›
Post-Weight Loss / Skin Removal
Also known as: body lift, skin excision, post-bariatric surgery
Removes excess skin following significant weight loss. Can involve multiple body areas — abdomen, thighs, arms, back. Often staged across procedures.
›
Popular combination
Mummy Makeover (Body)
Tummy tuck + liposuction, often combined with breast procedures
A combination of procedures to restore the body after pregnancy. The body component typically includes a tummy tuck and liposuction — often done alongside breast work.
